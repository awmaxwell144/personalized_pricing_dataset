JetBlue (JBLU) has since deleted the post and called it an employee's "error," but not before the internet flagged the response as a quiet admission of surveillance pricing, which is the controversial practice of using personal data to set individualized prices.

A JetBlue spokesperson apologized for this week's error in an email to MarketWatch and said its fares "are not determined by cached data or other personal information."

Instead, the airline said pricing is based on real-time availability and can change at any moment based on demand.
